# PawPal+ Core Knowledge Base

## 1. Breed-Specific Dog Care

### American Hairless Terrier
- **Trait**: No fur; skin is directly exposed.
- **Core Advice**: Must apply pet-safe sunscreen or wear protective clothing for outdoor activities to prevent sunburn.

### Golden Retriever
- **Trait**: Prone to obesity and joint stress.
- **Core Advice**: Strict weight management is required. Provide glucosamine supplements in senior years for hip support.

### French Bulldog
- **Trait**: Brachycephalic (short-nosed), prone to breathing issues.
- **Core Advice**: Never exercise in high heat; they are extremely susceptible to heatstroke and respiratory distress.

### Border Collie
- **Trait**: Extremely high intelligence and energy.
- **Core Advice**: Requires at least 2 hours of physical and mental stimulation daily to prevent destructive behavior.

### Samoyed
- **Trait**: Thick double coat.
- **Core Advice**: Frequent brushing to prevent matting. Never shave their back in summer as the coat protects against heat.

## 2. Breed-Specific Cat Care

### Siamese
- **Trait**: Highly social and vocal.
- **Core Advice**: Prone to depression if left alone. Requires at least 1 hour of active social interaction daily.

### Persian
- **Trait**: Long hair and flat face.
- **Core Advice**: Clean tear stains daily to prevent skin infections. Must be brushed daily to prevent tangles.

### Bengal
- **Trait**: High energy; loves climbing.
- **Core Advice**: Must provide vertical space (cat trees); otherwise, they may climb and damage curtains or furniture.

### Sphynx
- **Trait**: Hairless; skin produces significant oils.
- **Core Advice**: Requires weekly baths to remove oil buildup. Needs sweaters in winter and sunscreen in summer.

### Ragdoll
- **Trait**: Large size; extremely docile "floppy" personality.
- **Core Advice**: Lower pain sensitivity; owners must perform weekly body checks as the cat may not cry out when injured.

## 3. Dietary Restrictions — Toxic Foods (DO NOT FEED)

- **Grapes & Raisins**: Even a small amount can cause acute kidney failure in both dogs and cats.
- **Chocolate & Caffeine**: Contains theobromine, which causes heart tremors, seizures, and death.
- **Xylitol**: Common in sugar-free gum; causes a rapid drop in blood sugar and liver failure.
- **Onions & Garlic**: Destroys red blood cells, leading to hemolytic anemia.
- **Lilies**: Extremely toxic to cats; even licking the pollen can cause fatal kidney failure.

## 3. Dietary Restrictions — Toxic Foods (Additional)

- **Alcohol**: Even small amounts can cause vomiting, disorientation, and fatal respiratory depression.
- **Raw Dough / Yeast**: Expands in the stomach; produces alcohol as a byproduct, causing bloating and toxicity.
- **Macadamia Nuts**: Causes weakness, hyperthermia, and vomiting in dogs within 12 hours of ingestion.
- **Avocado**: Contains persin, which causes vomiting and diarrhea; the pit is also a choking hazard.

## 5. Routine Care Protocols

### Dental Care
- **Dogs & Cats**: Brush teeth 2–3 times per week using pet-safe toothpaste (never use human toothpaste — xylitol is toxic).
- **Professional Cleaning**: Recommended once a year under veterinary supervision.

## 5. Routine Care Protocols — Parasite Prevention
- **Fleas & Ticks**: Apply monthly topical or oral preventatives year-round in warm climates.
- **Heartworm**: Monthly preventative medication required; annual blood test to confirm negative status.
- **Deworming**: Puppies and kittens should be dewormed every 2 weeks until 12 weeks old, then monthly until 6 months.

## 5. Routine Care Protocols — Nail Trimming
- **Frequency**: Every 3–4 weeks for most breeds.
- **Caution**: Avoid cutting the quick (the blood vessel inside the nail); keep styptic powder nearby to stop bleeding if cut.

---

## 4. Veterinary Protocols — Vaccination Schedule
- **Core Vaccines**: Rabies is mandatory every 1 or 3 years.
- **Puppies & Kittens**: Start at 6–8 weeks old, repeating every 3–4 weeks until 16 weeks old.

## 4. Veterinary Protocols — Spay / Neuter Surgery
- **Pre-op Fasting**: No food for 12 hours before surgery; no water for 8 hours before.
- **Post-op Care**: Must wear an Elizabethan collar (E-collar) for 7–10 days to prevent licking the incision.
